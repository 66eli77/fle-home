No Internet required

KA Lite is open-source software that mimics the experience of an online user on Khan Academy for offline situations. With KA Lite, you can watch Khan Academy videos, do Khan exercises, and track student progress -- all without needing an Internet connection!

Progress tracking built in

Students can work their way through the content at their own pace, so that advanced learners aren't being held back, and less-advanced learners aren't being left behind. Teachers and parents can view student reports, so that class time can be used focusing on the individual learner's needs, rather than teaching to the middle, making the "flipped classroom" teaching model available to the off-line world.

Designed to fit your needs

KA Lite can be run as a personal installation on a single computer, or it can be configured as a local webserver, so that an entire classroom can watch videos and store their progress in one place. Want to deploy KA Lite in multiple classrooms or communities and sync your data? No problem. Learn more about this process.
