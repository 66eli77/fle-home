FLE Friends and Supporters

FLE wants to recognize our partners and collaborators for the invaluable support they have given us to accomplish our vision of a world in which every child enjoys a high-quality education that inspires him or her to become a life-long learner and a leader in his or her community. Alongside these organizations we have been able to design and implement innovative programs, making a bigger impact in the communities we all serve. Thank you all for your support!

Our Partners

Open Learning Exchange OLE International is committed to removing the barriers to learning so that all of our children enjoy learning that is open, free and meaningful, enabling them to become, in their own ways, productive, life-long learners and leaders which is a requirement for a sustainable world. The Foundation for Learning Equality is collaborating with OLE on ways to help make this shared vision a reality.

Khan Academy is a not-for-profit with the goal of changing education for the better by providing a free world-class education for anyone anywhere. The Foundation for Learning Equality is partnering with Khan Academy to play a key role in achieving this mission, by bridging the gap to offline and low-bandwidth communities using KA Lite.

Idaho Department of Corrections (IDOC) is part of the Khan in Idaho program, the FLE has partnered with the NNU Center of Innovation in Teaching and the CCA to bring KA Lite to inmates across 7 facilities of the Idaho Department of Corrections. This year-long study will examine the efficacy of Khan Academy content, and we hope it might serve as a launching pad to bring KA Lite to prisons across the nation.

High Tech High is a public charter school that serves a diverse, lottery-selected student population from around San Diego County.

The Akanksha Foundation is a non-profit organization with the vision to one day equip all students with the education, skills and character they need to lead empowered lives. We are piloting KA Lite in several Akanksha classrooms, with funding for the hardware provided by an Indian family foundation.
